Plagiarism

The internet has empowered us to find information on almost every topic. However, the ease of availability of information does not mean that we are free to use it in any way we like. Text, images and other information available on the Internet are often protected by copyright lawas and their use may require obtaining written permission from the copyright owner and acknowledging their source.

Plagiarism refers to an act of using others ideas and words without acknowledging the source of that information.

Plagiarism is a serious offence that results n sever penalties. In order to avoid plagiarism, one needs to obtain written permission from the copyright owner and the source when using information such as:

  • ideas or theories proposed by another person; any facts, statistics, images, graphs or any peice of information that is not common.
  • spoken or written quotationsof some other person.
